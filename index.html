The Simulation Logic

Diagnosis Before Prescription

Before designing a loyalty program, we must first address the systemic friction that erodes existing trust.

1. The Voice Audit

I analyzed real-world customer sentiment across Tasheel’s digital touchpoints. The goal was to find "Loyalty Leakage"—moments where customers intended to stay but were blocked by operational gaps.

2. Governance Focus

A CRM is not a database; it is a Governance Engine. It must safeguard Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) by ensuring that backend operations match the frontend brand promise.

3. The eXtra Advantage

Tasheel’s "Unfair Advantage" is its retail synergy with eXtra. We move beyond simple financing to create a closed-loop economy where financing drives retail, and retail loyalty drives financial compliance.

4. Strategic Objectives

To transition from "Reactive Service" to "Proactive Loyalty." We solve the problem before the customer feels the pain, utilizing intelligent CRM triggers and behavioral segmentation.

Competitive Moat

The eXtra Flywheel.

Most financial brands pay to acquire a customer. Tasheel is born inside the retail ecosystem. Our loyalty strategy leverages this to create a locked-in cycle of value.

Zero-CAC Acquisition

By utilizing eXtra's massive footfall and digital intent data, the CRM can predict financing needs before a loan application is even considered. We convert retail browsing into financial loyalty with zero external ad spend.

Closed-Loop Redemption

Loyalty points earned through Tasheel repayments are designed for redemption at eXtra. This ensures that the capital never truly leaves our ecosystem, creating a powerful incentive for on-time payments to fund the next upgrade.

Turning a single transaction into a 10-year financial relationship.
